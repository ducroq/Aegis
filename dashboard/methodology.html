How Aegis Works

Transparent, reproducible macro risk assessment

üéØ Core Philosophy

  • Data > News: Use direct economic data, not news commentary

üìä Risk Dimensions (5 total, weights sum to 1.0)

1. Recession Risk (30% weight)

Key Indicators:

  • Unemployment claims velocity (YoY % change) - leading indicator
  • ISM PMI regime cross detection (expansion ‚Üí contraction)
  • Dual yield curve (10Y-2Y + 3M‚Üí18M forward spread)

Philosophy: Rate of change > absolute values for leading signals

2. Credit Stress (25% weight)

Key Indicators:

  • High-yield spread velocity (70% weight) + level (30%)
  • Investment-grade (BBB) spreads
  • Bank lending standards survey
  • TED spread (interbank stress)

Philosophy: Credit markets lead equities in crises

3. Valuation Extremes (20% weight)

Key Indicators:

  • CAPE ratio (Shiller P/E)
  • Buffett indicator (Market Cap / GDP)
  • Forward P/E ratio

Philosophy: Valuations don't cause crashes, but high valuations amplify them

4. Liquidity Conditions (15% weight)

Key Indicators:

  • Fed funds rate trajectory
  • M2 money supply velocity
  • VIX (implied volatility)
  • Dollar liquidity proxies

Philosophy: "Don't fight the Fed" - liquidity drives markets

5. Positioning & Speculation (10% weight)

Key Indicators:

  • CFTC S&P 500 net speculative positioning (contrarian)
  • CFTC Treasury positioning (squeeze risk)
  • VIX futures positioning (complacency)

Philosophy: Extreme positioning creates vulnerability

üìà Historical Performance

Backtested: 300 months (2000-2024)

  • Historical Max: 5.55 (April 2020, COVID crash)
  • Major Crises Detected: 3 of 4 (2000-2002, 2008-2009, 2020)
  • False Positive Rate: 2.4% (19 alerts / 25 years)
  • Lead Time: 0-6 months (coincident indicator, not predictive)

System Limitations:

  • Coincident, not leading - detects crises in progress, doesn't predict them
  • Monthly sampling misses fast crashes (e.g., Feb-Mar 2020)
  • Valuation warnings alone don't trigger alerts (2000 dot-com missed)
